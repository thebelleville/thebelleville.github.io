The Belleville is a living and learning foundation for young adults with Autism Spectrum Disorder. Arranged as a purpose-built small village, the foundation is located in Austin County, Texas along a picturesque lake on a 66-acre plot of land.

The environment at The Belleville has been designed By Swiss Born New York Based Architect, Pino Pavese, to create a healthy and wholesome place for individuals with special needs. Incorporating acoustics, Spatial, and safety elements, the main structure is split into three wings: learning, activities, and research.

The Interior Architectural Design strikes a unique balance between the familiar and the stimulating as it introduces the outdoors as an additional story of the space. Floor to ceiling windows connect indoors and outdoors seamlessly. Natural materials such as white oak create a warm and tranquil atmosphere. Soft architectural lighting enhances the natural light to create a comfortable ambiance.
